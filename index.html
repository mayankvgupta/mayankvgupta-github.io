How I Work — Methodology
Every project follows the same four-phase loop — expanding outward to explore the problem space, then narrowing to deliver with intention.
01
Discover
I start by listening — to users, stakeholders, and data — before forming any opinions about the solution.
User interviews
Stakeholder sessions
Competitive audit
Usage analytics
02
Define
I synthesize findings into a clear problem statement — so the team agrees on what we're solving before how.
Synthesis + themes
Personas + JTBD
Problem statement
Success metrics
03
Design
I explore many directions quickly, then test with real users and iterate until evidence supports a direction.
Lo-fi sketches
Prototyping
Usability testing
Hi-fi iteration
04
Deliver
I hand off with full specs, stay close through build, and measure outcomes to inform the next cycle.
Eng handoff + spec
QA + polish
Launch + rollout

About

I work best on things that feel too complicated to design.

Hi, I'm Mayank — a Senior UX Designer & Researcher based in Mountain View, CA. I started as a front-end developer and made the leap into UX when I realized that the most important problems weren't engineering problems — they were human ones.

I hold an MS in Information Experience Design from Pratt Institute (GPA: 3.93), where I specialized in user research, accessibility, and information architecture. I believe that everyone should have the same experience while using digital products.

How I Work— 3 principles
01.
Research before pixels.
I don't open Figma until I understand the problem. User interviews, analytics, and competitive audits come first — the design follows from evidence, not intuition.
02.
Design for everyone.
Accessibility is not a feature you add at the end — it's a design constraint that makes products better for all users. I build it in from the first sketch.
03.
Stay close through build.
A great handoff isn't a wall. I stay in close contact with engineering through implementation, QA, and launch — because the gap between spec and shipped is where details disappear.
